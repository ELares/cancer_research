A commentary on ‘Advancements and current trends in tumor treating fields: a scientometric analysis’

 Dear Editor, 

Over the past decade, the field of clinical oncology has undergone a rapid development of diagnosis and therapeutic progress. Tumor treating fields (TTFields) are low-intensity intermediate-frequency alternating electric fields. Based on the current clinical study of TTFields in brain tumors, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) initially approved TTFields in 2015 for the treatment of newly diagnosed or relapsed glioblastoma multiforme (GBM) and in 2019 for the treatment of malignant pleural mesothelioma (MPM). We read the scientometric analysis of TTFields published on 13 February 2024. The first bibliometric study in TTFields gave us comprehensive and deep insights into current status. We raise some concerns about three details in this bibliometric paper after carefully reading.

First, we express our concern with the citation of HEPANOVA phase 2 in the introduction section of this scientometric analysis. As far as we know, HEPANOVA phase 2 (TTFields concomitant with solafenib vs. history control) did not reach the primary endpoint (objective reaction rate, ORR), and all responses are partial. HEPANOVA phase 2 enrolled 27 patients who were not candidates for surgery or local treatments. Based on the comparatively small size of the sample and negative therapeutic result, the possibility of acquiring a breakthrough grant from the FDA for TTFields plus immunotherapy is low. In fact, we checked the list of medical devices approved in the last 3 years and find NovoTTF-200T is not in the approval list of breakthrough devices.

Second, bibliometric methods offer new research fields and the identification of emerging hotspots. General analysis of keywords in Citespace shows novel hotspots and research output. Considering the rising amount of research in TTFields, we recommend an important hotspot of this progressing discipline: dosimetry of TTFields, which is not mentioned in this scientometric study. The EF-14 trial is the foundational research for establishing the concept of dosimetry of TTFields therapy. This study suggested that delivering higher doses of TTFields to the tumor bed can improve overall survival (OS). Determining the dose of TTFields is an essential step before applying it to the chest or abdomen malignant tumor. Heterogeneity of tumor and personalized planning are both two important issues to be assessed before we decide to list TTFields in our multiple discipline teamwork.

Last, the annual publication trend of TTFields before and after 2020 is different: inclining before 2020 and then descending in a plateau shape in the following 4 years. This sudden and continuous change should be ascribed to the emerging star of immunotherapy in oncology. And immunotherapy is continued to be the draw of cancer treatment and will attract more attention in the future. In the three tables (Tables 4, 5, and 6) of scientometric analysis, we noticed tumor microenvironment, T cell, appeared first in 2019. From then on, TTFields concomitant with immunotherapy is a highlighting direction in cancer research.

In this letter, we advocate that a scientometric review should present a much deeper analysis when the amount space of paper is occupied by tables and figures. As far as research in TTFields, dosimetry and combination with immunotherapy should be highlighted when further study is carried out in chest and abdominal malignant tumors.
