# Prior-predictive uncertainty: sim-tme headlines (#332)

Generated by `scripts/headline_uncertainty.py --headline sim-tme`. Propagates the same joint UNIFORM prior over the 11 PRCC rate constants (the ranges the PRCC #134 / Sobol #331 / Morris screen use) through the two spatial `sim-tme` headlines, extracted from one (costly, ~4 min) run per draw via `headline_sensitivity.run_sim_tme_observables`:
- **hypoxia**: the SDT-minus-RSL3 hypoxic-zone kill GAP (the kill-collapse asymmetry — SDT holds, RSL3 collapses);
- **immune**: SDT's pool-de-confounded immune kill rate `immune_kills / (total_tumor - ferroptosis_kills)` (bounded [0,1]).

## Method

- 100 Monte-Carlo draws from the joint uniform prior (seed 332, deterministic); 100 runs informed the hypoxia interval, 100 the immune interval.
- **Small-sample caveat:** sim-tme costs ~4 min/run, so this uses far fewer draws (100) than the single-cell ensemble (2000); the 2.5/97.5 TAILS are correspondingly less stable than the single-cell or Bliss intervals — read the median + the broad spread, not the exact bounds.
- **Prior-predictive (forward) uncertainty over UNIFORM priors — NOT a data-conditioned posterior** (blocked on the #330 GDSC data); PARAMETER, not STRUCTURAL, uncertainty.
